Before you start

What to know

Opportunities vary by partner organization. Each event lists its own requirements, so the details below are general guidance — always review the specific event before you go.

—Who can volunteer

Students and community members are welcome. Some events have a minimum age set by the partner organization, and volunteers under 18 may need parent or guardian permission. Group and family volunteering is often available.

—Age & guardian considerations

When an event has an age minimum or requires guardian permission, it will be stated on the event. For minors, a parent or guardian may need to provide consent and contact information.

—Service hours

Many opportunities can count toward school or club service-hour requirements. Let us know what your school needs and we'll do our best to provide appropriate documentation.

—Conduct & preparation

Follow partner guidelines and event leaders, treat animals and people with care, and arrive prepared. Each event notes what to wear or bring. If you need to cancel, let us know early so the spot can go to someone else.

—Accessibility & accommodations

Tell us about any accommodations that would help you participate, and we'll work with you and the partner organization where we can.

How it works

From sign-up to service

  1. 01

    Explore opportunities

    Browse the events calendar or sign up below to be matched with opportunities that fit you.

  2. 02

    Review the requirements

    Check the event's age requirement, location, timing, and what to bring or wear.

  3. 03

    Register

    Submit your information. For minors, include a parent or guardian where required.

  4. 04

    Get confirmation

    A coordinator follows up by email to confirm your spot and share final details.

  5. 05

    Prepare & serve

    Arrive ready, check in with the event leader, and complete your volunteer work.

  6. 06

    Keep going

    Come back for future opportunities — recurring volunteers make the biggest difference.

Who we partner with

A few kinds of partnerships.

We try to be useful, not in the way. Below is what working with us usually looks like.

01

Shelters, rescues & clinics

Volunteer days, supply drives matched to your current needs, and student help at adoption events. We follow your rules and orientation requirements.

02

Schools & student groups

Co-host a supply drive, run a student volunteer day, or invite us for an animal-welfare awareness session for a club or class.

03

Local businesses

Host a supply drop-off bin, donate supplies or services, sponsor an event, or amplify a campaign to your customers and staff.

04

Community & cultural groups

Help us share a supply drive with your network, host a collection at an event, or bring volunteers to a shared project.

Ways to support

Practical things partners can do.

Host a supply drive

Put a collection bin at your school, office, place of worship, or community event. We'll provide a list of items partner shelters need and pick the supplies up.

Coordinate a student volunteer day

We can help coordinate a one-time volunteer day for your students or staff at a partner organization, following their rules and orientation.

Sponsor an event

Help cover the cost of supplies, signage, or refreshments for a community event. We'll be transparent about what was used and what went to the partner shelter.

Share adoption information

Share posts from partner shelters about animals available for adoption. Reach matters, especially in a local network.

Support education & outreach

Invite us to speak to a club, class, or community group about animal welfare, responsible pet care, or how students can help.

Donate supplies or services

Donate pet food, blankets, leashes, toys, printing, design, or transport. In-kind support often makes the biggest difference for a small group.

About sponsorship: we don't offer naming rights, executive briefings, or any benefits that would be unusual for a small student-led organization. We're happy to credit partners on the site and in event recaps when they want it.

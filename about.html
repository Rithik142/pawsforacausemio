About us

A student-led group
supporting animal welfare in Michigan.

Paws For A Cause is a student-led animal welfare organization based in Michigan. We help students support local shelters, rescues, and community animal organizations through volunteer service, supply drives, adoption awareness, and outreach.

How we started

Started by students, in Okemos, Michigan.

Paws For A Cause was started in 2025 by a group of high school students in Okemos, Michigan: Tejas Valisetty, Rithik Parthasarathy, and Vaibhav Mahesha.

They had noticed that local shelters, rescues, and community animal groups often need more steady volunteer support than they can find, and that there were a lot of students nearby who wanted to help but did not know how to start.

So we built a student-led group that organizes practical service work: volunteer hours at partner shelters, supply drives, adoption awareness, and community outreach projects.

Okemos, MI · Founded 2025
Mission

Help students support animal welfare in their communities.

We organize student volunteers, run supply drives, and partner with local shelters and rescue organizations so that students have a practical way to help animals near them.

What we are not

A shelter, a rescue, or a vet clinic.

We do not house, transport, or treat animals ourselves. Our role is to support the partner organizations that do that work, and to bring more student hands and supplies their way.

What we value

A few things we try to stick to.

01

Service before recognition

Show up, do the work, and follow the partner organization's lead. The credit is not the point.

02

Responsible volunteering

We follow shelter rules, age and training requirements, and parent or guardian permission where needed.

03

Transparency

We share an honest picture of where we are. Small group, real work, growing slowly.

04

Respect for shelters and animals

Shelters and rescues know their animals best. We are there to support, not to direct.

05

Student leadership

Students plan and run our projects. We learn by doing, and we ask for guidance when we need it.

06

Community partnerships

We try to make it easier for shelters, schools, families, and community groups to work together.

How we work

A simple loop.

01

Listen

Ask partner shelters what they need this month.

02

Plan

Organize student volunteers and set dates and drop-off points.

03

Show up

Run the drive or event and deliver what was collected.

04

Recap

Share an honest update and look at what could go better.

Where we are now

An honest picture of our current stage.

Small, local, growing. We are a young organization. We have started by building relationships with partner shelters in mid-Michigan, completing volunteer orientations, and welcoming our first cohort of student volunteers. Anything you read on this site reflects work in progress, not a finished story. If you want a current update, please contact us.
